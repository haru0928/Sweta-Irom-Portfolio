About

Practicing from Imphal

Sweta is an architecture graduate from the Royal School of Architecture, Guwahati, now practicing as a visualization architect at Form & Space Architects in Imphal. She specializes in exterior and interior 2D and 3D visualization — turning a design intent into drawings a contractor can build from, and renders a client can trust.

Her undergraduate thesis, an Ecotherapy Centre on a 27-acre site in Imphal East, explored how architecture can connect people with nature to heal mental and physical health. That grounding is complemented by fieldwork: documentation studies in Poa Mecca, Hajo and in the Mishing and Panbari villages near Kaziranga, alongside an exposure programme on Laurie Baker's approach to architecture and building science. She has represented her school as a student volunteer and delegate at two national NASA (Architecture Students' Association) conventions, and was shortlisted twice at ANDC.

"A good drawing removes doubt. A good render removes it earlier."
