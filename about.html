About

Practicing from Imphal

Sweta is an architecture graduate from the Royal School of Architecture, Guwahati, looking to grow her design and architecture skills in a collaborative studio environment. Her focus is on 3D visualization and 2D drafting — turning a design intent into drawings a contractor can build from, and renders a client can trust.

That grounding comes partly from fieldwork: documentation studies in Poa Mecca, Hajo and in the Mishing and Panbari villages near Kaziranga, alongside an exposure programme on Laurie Baker's approach to architecture and building science. She has represented her school as a student volunteer and delegate at two national NASA (Architecture Students' Association) conventions, and was shortlisted twice at ANDC.

"A good drawing removes doubt. A good render removes it earlier."
